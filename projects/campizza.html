The Background

April 2019-June 2019

Camp Izza is a non-profit summer camp for children aged 5-11, located in Irvine, California. In order to promote social good, my group wanted to redesign the website for a better user experience. At the time, the website had many unclear affordances, gaps in information, and did not support recovery from mistakes.

The Problem

Information is difficult to find.

The information architecture of the website made it hard for users to find the information they were looking for. It often led users to remain "stuck" in their user flow and ultimately led them away from Camp Izza's services.

The UI is inconsistent

The visual aesthetic of the website was inconsistent. Thus, affordances were unclear for the user. Was the button clickable, or was it simply text? Some color combinations and fonts made reading near impossible in certain pages.

Website exploration is annoying.

There is no easy back button. Sometimes, user navigation is abruptly cut short because there's no button that takes the user back to where they are. If input information is wrong, users had to re-do an entire form because their input is not saved.

The Research

One of the first user research methods we used was competitive analysis. We named two direct competitors, Outta Bound Events and Camp James. The first is a non-profit organization that provides after-school/summer programs for children aged 5 to 14. The second is a summer camp for children aged 4 to 13.

Our indirect competitors are SuperCamp by Quantum Learning and Orange County Cal Ripken (OCCR). SuperCamp is aimed for older children, from sixth grade all the way to college students. They're focused on teaching classes about life skills. OCCR is an organization for children 3-18 that offers softball and baseball programs.

Looking at our competitors' websites, it is clear that each had better information hierarchy. In particular, Outta Bounds contained more information about each program in their pages and had a better navigation bar. Another advantage the sites had is a clear color palette. They stuck to a few colors and fonts which made it easier on the users' eyes. The competitive analysis solidified our goals and served as a confirmation that the problems we aimed for our redesign will help Camp Izza stand beside its competitors.

The next user research methods we used were interviews and surveys. We conducted our interviews on a total of 10 parents, our primary stakeholders, and posted our survey on Facebook groups for parents/guardians. Our survey was created using Google Survey, which garnered 20 responses, and we were able to utilize their built-in analytics for each question. We decided the best way to organize our findings is by creating affinity maps.

From our affinity maps, we decided to create our personas. We created a total of three with varying backgrounds and personality traits.

After finalizing our user base and uncovering their values, we moved onto dissecting the usability issues. We used user testing, accompanied by the think-aloud method, and had a post-task questionnaire prepared.
